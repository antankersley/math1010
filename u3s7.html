3.7 Volume and Surface Area

    \( \begin{equation} V = 1178 \text{ ft}^{3} \text { when full} \end{equation} \)

    \( \begin{equation} V = 883.6 \text{ ft}^{3} \text{ when 75% full} \end{equation} \)

  2. You plan to wrap the 55” TV you bought for your partner for Christmas. The box it came in is 56” long, 8” deep and 36” tall. How many square feet of wrapping paper will you need to wrap it?

    3. Surface Area of a Rectangular Prism

    SA = 2LW + 2LH + 2WH

    SA = 2 * 56 * 8 + 2 * 56 * 36 + 2 * 8 * 36

    \( \begin{equation} SA = 5504 \text{ in}^{2} \end{equation} \)

    12 in = 1 ft

    \( \begin{equation} 12^{2} \text{ in}^{2} = 1^{2} \text{ ft}^{}2 \end{equation} \)

    \( \begin{equation} 144 \text{ in}^{2} = 1 \text{ ft}^{2} \end{equation} \)

    \( \begin{equation} \frac{5504 \text{ in}^{2}}{144 \text{ in}^{2}} = 38.2 \text{ ft}^{2} \end{equation} \)

    \( \begin{equation} SA = 38.2 \text{ ft}^{2} \end{equation} \)

  3. The circumference of a standard basketball is approximately 75 centimeters. The diameter of a basketball goal rim is 18 inches.
    1. How much bigger is the rim than the basketball?

      2. Circumference of a Circle

      The circumference of the basketball is 75 cm.

      1 in = 2.54 cm

      \( \begin{equation} \frac{75 \text{ cm}}{2.54 \text{ cm}} = 29.53 \text{ in} \end{equation} \)

      The circumference of the basketball is 29.53 in.

      \( \begin{equation} C = \pi d \end{equation} \)

      The circumference of the rim is \( \begin{equation} C = \pi * 18 \text{ in} \end{equation} \)

      C = 56.55 in

      The circumference of the rim is 56.55 in.

      56.55 - 29.53 = 27.02

      The rim is 27.02 in larger in circumference than the basketball.

    2. How many square centimeters of rubber does it take to cover the outer surface of a standard basketball?

      3. Surface Area of a Sphere

      \( \begin{equation} SA = 4 \pi r^{2} \end{equation} \)

      The diameter of the sphere is \( \begin{equation} \frac{75}{ \pi} \text{ cm} \end{equation} \)

      d = 23.9 cm

      \( \begin{equation} r = \frac{23.9}{2} \end{equation} \)

      r = 11.95 cm

      \( \begin{equation} SA = 4 \pi * 11.95^{2} \end{equation} \)

      \( \begin{equation} SA = 1794.51 \text{ cm}^{2} \end{equation} \)

      It will take \( \begin{equation} 1794.51 \text{ cm}^{2} \end{equation} \) to cover the outer surface of a standard basketball.

    3. How many cubic inches of air does it take to fill a standard basketball?

      4. Volume of a Sphere

      \( \begin{equation} V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^{3} \end{equation} \)

      The radius of the basketball is 11.95 cm

      1 in = 2.54 cm

      \( \begin{equation} \frac{11.95 \text{ cm}}{2.54 \text{ cm}} = 4.7 \text{cm} \end{equation} \)

      \( \begin{equation} V = \frac{4}{3} \pi * 4.7^{3} \end{equation} \)

      \( \begin{equation} V = 434.89 \text{ in}^{3} \end{equation} \)

      The volume of the basketball is \( \begin{equation} 434.89 \text{ in}^{3} \end{equation} \) .
